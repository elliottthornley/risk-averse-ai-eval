\paragraph{Llama 3.1 8B baseline on the OOD risk-aversion suite.}
We evaluated Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct on the same four paper-facing OOD risk-aversion datasets used for the Qwen baselines: medium-stakes validation ($n=200$), high-stakes test ($n=1000$), astronomical-stakes deployment ($n=1000$), and steals test ($n=1000$). We kept the evaluation protocol as close as possible to the current Qwen 3 8B default setup by using the unchanged prompt and direct calls to the standard \texttt{evaluate.py} evaluator, with vLLM decoding parameters temperature $0.6$, top-$p$ $0.95$, top-$k$ $20$, seed $12345$, max new tokens $4096$, reasoning max tokens $800$, batch size $4$, prefix caching enabled, and saved responses enabled throughout.

Parse rates were $100\%$ on all four datasets. On medium-stakes validation, the model cooperated on $36.5\%$ of situations and rebelled on $63.5\%$, with best-CARA and best-linear rates of $35.0\%$ and $59.5\%$. On the high-stakes test, it cooperated on $26.6\%$ of situations and rebelled on $73.4\%$, with best-CARA and best-linear rates of $25.3\%$ and $65.0\%$. On astronomical-stakes deployment, it cooperated on $36.6\%$ of situations and rebelled on $63.4\%$, with best-CARA and best-linear rates of $34.0\%$ and $56.1\%$. On steals test, it cooperated on $60.6\%$ of situations and stole on $39.4\%$, with best-CARA and best-linear rates of $53.8\%$ and $54.2\%$.

This baseline does not appear to be driven by a trivial first-option bias. Across the $2007$ two-option situations in these four datasets, the model chose the first option $1017$ times and the second option $990$ times, for a first-option rate of $50.67\%$. Under the unchanged prompt, the model's outputs remained extremely short, with mean generated length of roughly three tokens per example, so this baseline should be understood as a clean unchanged-prompt comparison rather than a reasoning-visible Llama-specific prompting protocol.
